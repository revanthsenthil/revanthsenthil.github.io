ROS TFs: A reflection of using TFs on the Boston Dynamics Spot

Posted on: 07/28/2023

ROS TFs are a way to keep track of coordinate frames in ROS. They provide a mean to understand of the position and orientation of objects in a 3D space, along with the relation between different coordinate frames. Working with the Boston Dynamics Spot robot quadruped over the Summer, I gained invaluable experience in using TFs and appreciating their presence in the robotics world. Though TFs are not among the first tools that are useful for any robotics developers, when implementing a perception subcomponent in your system or a combination of a bunch of links on the robot, TFs become crucial in building and debugging both in real life and in simulation.
